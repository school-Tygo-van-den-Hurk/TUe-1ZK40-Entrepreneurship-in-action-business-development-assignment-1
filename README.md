# Template

This is a repository that will function as a template for future school projects.

## Assignment 

### Objective

The objective of this assignment is to identify and evaluate market opportunities for the technology of your choice as presented by HighTechXL.  
A **market opportunity** is defined as the combination of an application of your (technical) abilities for a specific set of customers.

You will perform the first two steps of the **Market Opportunity Navigator (MON)**:

- **Step 1:** Use Worksheet 1 to generate a market opportunity set  
- **Step 2:** Use Worksheet 2 to evaluate market opportunity attractiveness

### Approach

Follow the steps and instructions provided in the workshop.

#### Step 1: Generate a Market Opportunity Set (Worksheet 1)

- Based on a first brainstorming exercise in class, you have already defined a number of market opportunities
- Search online databases such as:
  - Patent databases
  - Industry lists  
  to expand your market application set with **at least 5 additional market applications**
- Develop a prompt to use in **Microsoft Copilot** to help identify additional market applications
- Present an overview of **all identified market applications** and indicate whether they resulted from:
  - Brainstorming
  - Online search
  - Copilot

#### Step 2: Evaluate Market Opportunity Attractiveness (Worksheet 2)

- Every student selects **one market opportunity** to evaluate using Worksheet 2
- For each of the **five selected market opportunities**:
  - Use online resources and Copilot to gather input for the six evaluation dimensions:

#### Evaluation Dimensions

**Potential**
- Compelling reason to buy
- Market volume
- Economic viability

**Challenge**
- Implementation obstacles
- Time to revenue
- External risks

- Conduct **1 to 3 customer discovery interviews** to validate the problem  
  (use the tips and tricks from the workshop on **Monday 25 November**)

### Deliverables

#### 1. Written Report (10–15 pages)

Include:

- An overview of all identified market applications, indicating the source:
  - Brainstorm
  - Online search
  - Copilot
- A detailed assessment of **five** (or **four**, depending on group size) market opportunities using MON Worksheet 2  
  **Evidence for ranking is essential**
- A conclusion stating which market opportunity to focus on
- An appendix containing:
  - References and data sources
  - Microsoft Copilot prompt(s) and results
  - Overview of interviewees and summary of interview insights

#### 2. Slide Deck

- Presentation to be held in **Week 4**
- Summarize the key insights from the report

### Assessment Rubric

#### Report (80%)

##### Identifying Market Opportunities (20%)

**Sufficient (6):**
- More than **15 market opportunities** generated
- Clear understanding of a market opportunity (application + specific customer set)
- Opportunities reflect awareness of the technology’s core abilities
- Use of patent databases and industry sources
- Development and use of a Copilot prompt

**Good (Sufficient Plus):**
- Market opportunities are **specific**  
  (e.g. *steel plant using blast furnace* instead of *all steel plants*)
- Use of **multiple** patent and industry databases

##### Opportunity Evaluation & Interviews

**Sufficient (6):**
- Required number of opportunity evaluations  
  (1 per team member)
- **1–3 customer interviews** conducted
- Each worksheet component is well substantiated with appropriate sources
- Final selected market opportunity logically follows from analysis

**Good (Sufficient Plus):**
- More than the required number of interviews
- Proactive and creative evidence gathering

#### Progress Presentation (20%)

##### Slides and Content (10%)

**Sufficient (6):**
- Properly structured presentation
- Convincing evidence for market opportunity evaluation

**Good (Sufficient Plus):**
- Visually appealing slides
- Effective use of quotes and online market research

##### Presentation Style and Q&A (10%)

**Sufficient (6):**
- Clear and convincing presentation
- Satisfactory answers to questions

**Good (Sufficient Plus):**
- Demonstrates in-depth understanding of the technology and its abilities

### Source

Gruber, M., & Tal, S. (2017). *Where to play: 3 steps for discovering your most valuable market opportunities*. Pearson UK.
